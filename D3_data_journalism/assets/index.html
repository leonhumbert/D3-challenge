Analysis of Health Risks - Case 2014 on Particular Demographics

Trends Uncovered Between Health Risks and Poverty, Age and Income

The interactive D3 enabled scatterplot above shows the relationship between a chose pair of variables at a state level. Click on a variable on each axis to see how their relationship.

Some interesting trends:

  • A positive relationship between all three health risks analyzed and poverty is observed. Southern states show the highest rates of poverty and obesity, smoking rate, and low health coverage.
  • The age demographic is squarely within the middle age range from age 30-45.
  • There is a negative correlation between Lacks Healthcare and Household Income, so wealthier states tend to have more healthcare coverage.
  • Smoking has a positive correlation with poverty, none with age, and a negative correlation with household income.
  • Obesity also has a positive correlation with poverty, none with age, and a negative correlation with household income.
